A little bit about me

I am a student at Business Academy Aarhus on Multimedia Design and Communucation programme. For my specialisation I picked frontend as developing working products brings me lots of satisfaction and is something that I am most passionate about.

Who I am

Born in Lubin, Poland in 2001. What pushed me into moving abroad was my curiosity about the world and desire to meet new people.

I am always motivated and eager to gain new experience. I am quick to adapt to the new situations and groups. Lerning fast is also not a problem for me. I am that kind of person who has a plan if the backup plan for a backup plan wouldn't work but when I see a good opportunity I am quick to make a decission.

My skillset

I am on the frontend specialisation and I am most passionate about programming. Through the last semesters I have been mostly working mostly with HTML and CSS. Even though I have been introduced to JavaScript just recently I feel this is something I want to work with. I also had some oportunities to work with Python and C++ and I am planning on mastering those languages in the future as well.

While my main focus lays on developing my programming skills I also care a lot about creating best possible design. Whether it is a layout of the website or a piece of art I have to create, I always try to make it look perfect. In order to do that I work mostly with Adobe Xd and Adobe Photoshop but I am also familiar with other Adobe programs like Illustrator, Animate and Premiere Pro.

What I am passionate about

Well, about frontend of course! But beside that I really love playing Dungeons & Dragons. It may be just a game but a lot of creative and tactical thinking is needed to actually play it. I am known for always finding a way out from difficult situations and surviving whatever is thrown at me.

I play a lot of different games both multplayer where I have to cooperate with strangers and singleplayer for boosting my imagination.

As they say: "a healthy mind in a healthy body", so I never limited myself to just sitting in front of computer.

I was part of the airsoft group for a few years back in Poland. I'm not going to lie, I was terrible at aiming. Fortunately I was pretty good at navigating which was crucial skill in huge forests, especially at night with no light source. As one of the older members I often helped with organising events and creating tactics for longer games.

I have also trained Krav Maga, Takwondo and Karate Kyokushin. Most of the time I was the one collecting all of the punches but small inconveniences has never stopped me from doing what I want to.

The projects I have worked on

In Business Academy Aarhus the ability of cooperating in groups is of great importance. Most of the work I heve done were group projects nad I always made shure to contribute even to the parts I do not specialise in. I never pass the opportunity to learn new things!

How I work on the projects

In a group work there are almost never situtions where only one person is working on certian element and working together always holds a lot of challanges. Using right tools is extremely impoertant here and I am always quick to adapt knowledge about new ones.

The main tools I use are Adobe Xd for creating prototypes with my group and GitHub for coding together. Google Drive is perfect for sharing all of the files and Trello for creating action plan. For bigger projects I use platforms like Miro to map the ideas. As no project will go without ussability testing I use Loom for in-person tests and Lookback for online ones.

Every group has their own preferences when it comes to using tools and I am always quick to adapt to the new working environment.
